This project analyzes the construction and application of geometric surfaces and volumes over irregular domains. It addresses three primary methodologies.

First, it examines blending Bézier patches for multi-sided surface modeling. The principle involves filling $n$-sided holes while maintaining $G^1$ or $G^2$ continuity. The process begins by constructing a spider-web control net for $n$-sided domains. Next, it establishes corner control nets for each vertex through coordinate averaging. The system then applies local constrained sweep parameterization for each edge. Finally, it calculates Gregory corner blending functions to merge the individual patches.

Second, it explores generalized Bézier volumes over simple convex polyhedra. The principle targets full volumetric representation for isogeometric analysis and additive manufacturing. The process starts with building a polyhedral control net composed of overlapping semi-tensor product nets. It then defines local parameterization using 3D Wachspress coordinates. Following this, the method assigns face and edge weights to Bernstein polynomials. It allocates these weighted polynomials across distinct control points. The final step constructs face Bézier ribbons and assembles them into the complete generalized volume.

Third, it outlines $C^0$ generalized Coons patches for high-order cage deformation. The principle seeks to provide flexible deformation across arbitrary, non-manifold topologies. The process reparameterizes standard Coons patches utilizing generalized barycentric coordinates. It extends this formulation from four-sided domains to $n$-sided polygons. The framework further generalizes to high-dimensional and non-manifold spaces. It applies this mapping to update object coordinates, achieving high-order cage-based deformation.
